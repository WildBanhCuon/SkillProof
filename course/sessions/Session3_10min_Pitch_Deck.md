# 10 min Pitch Deck — SkillProof (oral script)

**Total:** ~10 minutes · Bullet points to read aloud.

---

## Present the idea (~5 sec)

- **Catch phrase:**  
  “Too many junior applicants, not enough proof. SkillProof helps companies turn messy applications into verified, job-ready talent shortlists.”

---

## What it solves? (~1 min)

- Junior tech hiring fails on **volume + low signal**: hundreds of similar CVs, slow screening, inconsistent decisions.
- HR and hiring managers spend weeks filtering noise instead of interviewing the right people.
- Many tools use **AI on CVs** to pre-filter candidates and claim to reduce bias.
- In practice, those models still learn from **historical hiring data** (who was hired before), so patterns like gender or school prestige can persist even when the goal is “fair” screening.
- **SkillProof shifts the decision to skills proof:**
  - improve the job ad so the right people apply,
  - run **role-specific technical tests** (coding + multiple choice),
  - grade with **transparent rubrics**, not opaque keyword scores.
- Outcome: a **defensible shortlist** — who to interview, and why, with evidence.

**One-liner:**  
“We don’t ask ‘does this CV look good?’ — we ask ‘can this person do the job?’”

---

## Who would use it and why? (~1 min)

- **Who pays:** Head of Talent, HR Lead, or Founder (companies **50–500 employees** worldwide).
- **Who uses it daily:** recruiters / talent partners (screening) + hiring managers (validate fit).
- **Why they buy:**
  - too many applications per junior role,
  - need faster time-to-shortlist,
  - need evidence they can defend to managers and leadership,
  - want fairer, skills-based decisions instead of CV storytelling.
- **Job they hire us for:**  
  “When I post a junior role and get flooded with applications, help me produce an interview-ready shortlist quickly with confidence.”

---

## Market evaluation — competitors (~1 min)

- **Market size (base case, scenario-based):**
  - TAM ≈ **USD 5.25B ARR** · SAM ≈ **USD 1.84B ARR** · SOM (12–24 mo) ≈ **USD 9.2M ARR**
  - Focus wedge: **junior tech hiring evidence**, not all HR software.
- **Substitutes / competitors (types):**
  - **ATS + AI CV screening** — fast, but CV-centric; weak on role-specific proof.
  - **Generic coding test platforms** — tests exist, but often not generated from *this* job ad.
  - **AI interview / async video tools** — scale screening, less transparent rubric evidence for juniors.
  - **Manual process** — spreadsheets + manager gut feel; slow, hard to audit.
- **Our differentiation:**
  - one workflow: job quality → assessment → ranked evidence,
  - practice vs application tests are **different** (anti-cheat),
  - scoring is explainable (dimensions + rubric), not a black-box “match %.”

**Closing line:**  
“We’re not replacing every HR tool — we own the wedge between ‘posted a junior role’ and ‘here’s who to interview.’”

## Revenue model (~1 min) + First-100-customers plan (~1 min)

### Revenue model

- **Model:** annual **subscription** (forfait) tiered by **company size** + hiring usage.
- **Indicative tiers:**
  - 50–100 employees: **USD 3k–5k / year**
  - 100–250 employees: **USD 7.5k–12k / year**
  - 250–500 employees: **USD 15k–25k / year**
- **Who pays vs who uses:** budget owner = HR/founder; daily users = recruiters + hiring managers.
- **Willingness-to-pay:** customers fund us by cutting manual screening time, fragmented tools, and weak interviews with poor-fit candidates.
- **Cheapest validation:** **paid pilots** (e.g. 10 companies, one live role each) → measure pilot-to-paid conversion + hours saved + shortlist quality.

### First-100-customers plan

- **Phase 1 (0–20):** founder-led outbound + warm intros → paid pilots.
- **Phase 2 (20–60):** case studies + LinkedIn proof → repeatable outbound.
- **Phase 3 (60–100):** referrals + light partners; tighten ICP (50–500, junior tech).
- **Motion:** hybrid **product-led + sales-assisted** (demo value fast, human help to close).
- **CAC logic:** track `sales + marketing spend / new paying customers`; target payback **< 12 months**.

## Risks (~1 min)

We name risks openly — each with mitigation:

| Risk | Likelihood (1–5) | Mitigation (short) |
|---|---:|---|
| **Market:** no one pays despite interest | 4 | Paid pilots, ROI metrics (time saved, interview quality), tiered pricing |
| **Technical:** AI pipeline fragile at scale / bad inputs | 3 | Guardrails, validation, model routing, monitoring, human review on low confidence |
| **Adoption:** HR won’t change workflow | 4 | Wrap existing process (export to ATS), fast onboarding, HR + hiring manager kickoff |

- **Trade-off we own:** **quality-at-speed** — fast first-pass grading, deeper review only when the model is uncertain.
- **Closing:** validating through pilots with real metrics, not assumptions alone.

## Live demo (~3 min)

**Say while you click — happy path:**

1. **HR side:** open a published junior role (e.g. frontend / WordPress).
2. **Check listing** → show AI flags on the job ad + skills matrix.  
   *“Bad ads attract the wrong crowd — we fix that first.”*
3. Show **candidate apply flow** → required profile fields before the real test.  
   *“We collect what HR needs before assessment.”*
4. **Practice test** → different question set (candidate can learn the format).  
   *“Practice doesn’t leak answers to the real application.”*
5. **Application test** → submit → grading → result with dimension scores.
6. **HR results** → ranked candidates, recommendation (ready now / trainable / at risk).  
   *“This is the shortlist evidence hiring managers need.”*
7. Optional: candidate notification (interview invited / declined).

**Demo one-liners:**
- “Role-calibrated assessments, not a generic test bank.”
- “Skills judged on rubrics — comparable across all applicants.”
- “From messy applications to verified shortlist in one workflow.”
